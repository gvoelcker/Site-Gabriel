This last Monday I presented my dissertation and had the pleasure of concluding another cycle: my masters in accounting and finance. The work is entitled: “An Analysis of Risk-Taking in Family Firms”, and I was oriented by Prof. PhD. Clea B. Macagnan.

The main objective of the research is to empirically test whether family firms are more risk averse than non-family firms. The empirical procedures can be divided into two stages. First, I performed tests to compare the average of variables differentiating among family firms and non family firms(62.5% of the tests indicated that they behave differently). Then, I tested my hypothesis using OLS and Random Effects. The results point to a more conservative behaviour from family firms towards risk-taking.

I used a sample of brazilian firms, with data from 2010 to 2017, obtaining 1188 observations. There were two main sources of information that I used. The first is Economática, which can be introduced as the brazilian Compustat. Almost all variables were downloaded from this software, available to me as a Unisinos student. But I also need all the information about shareholders, families and board of directors, which must be disclosed by all companies listed in the Brazilian stock market.

There are two ways of obtaining this information. The first is the more troublesome one: downloading all the available data at the source. In this case, one would have to download manually for each year and each company of the desired sample.

Luckily, a RStudio package has been developed that quickly downloads all the available data upon setting some parameters: GetDFPData. It is possible to obtain all the data via RStudio or here. If one is interested in obtaining data from Brazilian companies, this is the best free source of data available, in my opinion. All the information is in English.
